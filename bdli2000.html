Black & Decker Li2000 Disassembly

Introduction

For my CAD & Machine Components Class, ENG ME 359, I was tasked with disassembling, analyzing, and modeling the Black & Decker Li2000 Rechargeable Screwdriver. A central goal of this project was to apply all of the knowledge and skills I had learned in class. I applied my knowledge of gear-train analysis to determine important properties of epicyclic gear train and I modeled the gear train using Computer Aided Design (CAD). Further, this project gave me a hands-on sense for how real products are designed and what drives the design process such as Design for Manufacturing and Assembly (DMFA).

Disassembly

The first step in the project was to disasseble the product in order to examine and analyze the components inside. Before disassebling the product, however, I familiarized myself with the device and how it worked.
Having familiarized myself with the standard operation of the product, I began the disassebly. First, I removed the screws holding the outer casing in place. I removed the shaft on which the handle moves and found the locking mechanism which allows the user to adjust the handle to their desired position. Next, I removed the outer casing and took note of the adjustable switch which is used to active the screw driver and choose in what direction it rotates. With the outer casing removed, I continued the disassembly by removing a pin which holds the motor in place and prying the motor casing open. Inside I found two epicyclic gear trains, each with three planetary gears, one sun gear, a planetary carrier, and a ring gear which was built into the casing around the gear train.
